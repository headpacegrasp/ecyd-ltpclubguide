What is ECYD?

ECYD stands for Encounters, Convictions, and Your Decisions. It is an international Catholic association of adolescents affiliated with the Regnum Christi movement. Members make a pledge of friendship with Christ and each other to build a new world according to the Gospel.

Through different experiences of encounter, adolescents acquire firm life convictions and learn to make free and responsible decisions according to Gospel criteria.

What is the LTP?

The ECYD Leadership Training Program is an established, internationally recognized tool for Catholic character formation. The boys' program forms young Catholic men ages 8–15 through a structured, high-impact curriculum that develops faith-filled, morally courageous leaders.

By fostering a consistent brotherhood and culture of virtue, the LTP ensures boys are anchored in Christ and prepared to become future leaders.

The “Little Trinity” Session Structure

Every 90-minute LTP session follows a consistent three-part format for holistic spiritual, character, and physical development:

15–20 min

1. Spiritual Anchor

  • ☞ Hero Saint & Scripture verse
  • ☞ Opening prayer sets reverent tone
  • ☞ Sainthood as attainable goal
  • ☞ God's Word as practical guidance
30–40 min

2. Formation Lesson

  • ☞ Mentor-filtered story or video
  • ☞ Guided discussion on virtue
  • ☞ Personal reflection → application
  • ☞ Concrete, memorable examples
25–35 min

3. Activity & Community

  • ☞ Team challenge embodying virtue
  • ☞ Short debrief naming virtue aloud
  • ☞ Virtue Nominations (“Donut”)
  • ☞ Take-home challenge assigned

The ECYD Formation Pathway

A strong club meeting moves boys through a clear four-step formation journey:

1

Encounter

Welcome by name. Prayer, saint image, Scripture. Open the heart through story.

2

Conviction

Facts to personal meaning. Direct questions. Name the virtue in livable language.

3

Decision

One concrete practice. Connect to home, school, parish. Believable next step.

4

Mission

Service, witness, deeper parish life. Confession, Mass, altar service, retreats.

Leader Translation: Name the virtue. Debrief the activity. Invite the sacramental life. Message parents the same day.

Best Practices

For Missionaries: Before, During & After

BEFORE

  • Pray by name for each boy
  • Prepare materials & assign roles
  • Rehearse transitions
  • Set up saint image & supplies

DURING

  • Short instructions, use names often
  • Move toward quieter boys
  • Make discussion concrete
  • Model upbeat, calm, never sarcastic tone

AFTER

  • Debrief: Where was virtue visible?
  • Note boys needing follow-up
  • Send parent message same day
  • Record attendance & moments

For the RC Missionary Priest

RC Missionary Priest Moments: Opening blessing tied to virtue, 2-minute catechetical connection, chapel visit or confession invitation, commissioning blessing. After: identify boys and families needing encouragement.
What Boys Should Feel: Fatherhood (safe, strong presence), Peace (God is near), Clarity (simple spiritual truth), Invitation (a real call to sacramental life).

The LTP Boys' Honor Code

Read aloud on the first meeting. Keep visible. Return to it when the room slips.

RESPECT

Build each other up. Laugh with, not at each other.

HONOR

Personal sharing is never turned into a weapon. Live saintly virtues inside and outside club.

ASK

No grades here. Honest questions without fear of being mocked.

NO BRO MASKS

No one is too cool to be here. Authenticity matters more than image.

Mentor Posture: Smile first, use names often. Praise publicly, correct privately. Move toward the quieter boy. Keep prayer and play equally intentional. Never use sarcasm or ridicule. If culture slips: slow down, restate the norm, reset with prayer, continue without shaming.

Semester Setup & Saint Partners

Saint Card Partnership

At the start of each semester, assign every boy a patron saint who accompanies him throughout the cycle:

  • Prepare saint cards with name, image, feast day, and a short bio
  • Place all cards in a bowl at Session 1
  • Each boy draws a card — this is his personal patron for the semester
  • Boys learn about their saint and bring one fact to share each week
  • Consider 3D-printing saint relics or medals as tangible reminders
  • Print enough for every boy plus extras for visitors

Icebreakers & Activities Bank

🎲 Icebreakers

🤔

Two Truths & a Lie 10 min

Boys break into groups of five. Each tells two truths and one lie. Group guesses the lie. Great for first sessions or welcoming new boys.

🎈

My Brother’s Keeper Balloons

Each boy puts his name on a balloon. Music plays — keep ALL balloons up, but you can’t touch your own! If yours hits the ground, you’re out. Area shrinks each round.

💡 Debrief: We are called to watch out for each other, not just ourselves.
🧟

Zombie Infection Outdoor

Tag-style game with escalating teamwork. One “zombie” starts — tagged players join the horde. Last survivor wins. High-energy outdoor reset.

🎭

Charades Saints & Bible

Act out saints, virtues, or Bible stories. Teams compete to guess fastest. Builds confidence and Catholic knowledge simultaneously.

🛄

Toilet Tag High Energy

Standard freeze tag with a twist: when tagged, you freeze in a “toilet” pose. A teammate must “flush” you (push your arm down) to unfreeze. Hilarious and keeps everyone moving.

⚔️ Featured Activities

🛡

Armor of God Defense Game

Spiritual Warfare • Teamwork • Prayer

Two teams face off across a center line. Team Righteous wields paper-plate shields inscribed with Scripture. Team Unrighteous throws soft balls labeled with sins and temptations.

📜 Rules:
  1. Team Righteous deflects balls with Scripture plates
  2. If hit → kneel and pray the St. Michael Prayer to rejoin
  3. Teammates must surround and defend the kneeling boy
  4. Hit 3 times → stay down for the round
  5. Timer-based: if >1 boy is down when time expires, Team Unrighteous wins
💡 Debrief: Spiritual warfare is real. We need the armor of God AND the prayers of our brothers.
⚽

Saint Names Countdown

Knowledge • Pressure • Quick Thinking

A fast-paced circle game that builds Catholic knowledge under friendly pressure.

📜 Rules:
  1. Stand in a circle with one ball
  2. Non-dominant hand only
  3. Say a saint’s name → throw to someone else
  4. 10 seconds to name a saint or sit down
  5. No repeats allowed
  6. Last one standing = Saint Champion 🏆
💡 Debrief: The saints are our friends — the more we know them, the more they can help us.
🔎

Relics of the Saints Scavenger Hunt

Exploration • Teamwork • Saint Knowledge

An adventure-style hunt that brings the saints to life through physical discovery.

📜 Setup:
  1. 2 teams, 3 saints each, 30-minute timer
  2. 1 adult mentor + 1 junior mentor per team
  3. Hide 3D-printed saint relics around the area
  4. Teams search, find relics, and return
  5. Debrief each relic and its associated saint
  6. Close with the weekly challenge
💡 Tip: Use hourglass timers for visual urgency. 3D-print relics for a tangible, collectible experience.
🕯

Adoration Experience

Prayer • Reverence • Encounter

The most spiritually impactful moment of the semester. Create sacred space for a genuine encounter with Christ.

📜 Flow:
  1. Begin with a short video lesson on Adoration
  2. Follow with a skit or small group discussion
  3. Lead boys to the Crucifix with music, incense, and dimmed lights
  4. Keep it reverent and brief (10–15 min for younger boys)
  5. Guide with simple prayers — give space for silence
💡 This can be the most impactful moment of the semester. Give it space and silence.

Team Challenge Activities

👁

Commander’s Orders

Trust • Patience • Communication

One partner is blindfolded. The other provides verbal instructions through an obstacle course. No touching — voice only.

📜 Rules:
  1. Pair up: one blindfolded, one guide
  2. Guide uses only voice to navigate obstacles
  3. Communicate clearly and calmly
  4. Cross the finish line → celebrate!
  5. Discuss: What strategies worked?
💡 Leaders must sometimes lead and sometimes trust and follow. Clear direction and broader vision are key.
🤝

Cooperation Challenge

Cooperation • Communication • Trust

The whole team must move from Point A to Point B — but each member is handicapped differently.

📜 Setup:
  1. Line up single file at starting point
  2. Bind hand-to-hand between 1st & 2nd boy
  3. Bind leg-to-leg between 2nd & 3rd, alternating
  4. Blindfold every other team member
  5. Walk together to the destination
  6. First team arriving safely wins
💡 Everyone has strengths and weaknesses. Success comes through complementing and supporting each other.

Points & Awards System

A well-designed points system transforms the LTP from a weekly meeting into a living, competitive, and rewarding experience. Boys thrive when effort is visible, recognized, and celebrated. Track points weekly on a visible leaderboard so boys can see progress.

Point Categories

📋

Attendance & Punctuality

Up to 15 pts/week

10 pts — Present at the session
5 bonus pts — Arrived on time (before opening prayer)
20 bonus pts — Attended all 8 sessions in a semester. This rewards faithfulness and commitment, which are themselves virtues.

⭐

Virtue in Action

5–20 pts per instance

5 pts — Caught practicing a virtue (helping, sharing, patience). Missionaries should actively look for these moments.
10 pts — Donut Nomination by a peer. The most powerful tool because it teaches boys to notice goodness in each other.
15 pts — Completed the Take-Home Challenge and can share what happened.
20 pts — Brought a friend to LTP or completed service outside the club (verified by parent).

🏆

Games & Competitions

5–25 pts

5 pts — Full participation (effort matters more than winning)
10 pts — Winning team in the weekly activity
15 pts — Individual standout (best communicator, most encouraging captain)
25 pts — Winning team in a semester-long competition

🙏

Spiritual Growth

10–30 pts

10 pts — Memorized the Scripture verse of the week
15 pts — Consistently completed the weekly prayer challenge
20 pts — Attended Mass, confession, or adoration outside LTP
30 pts — Shared a testimony, led a prayer, or mentored a younger boy. This is the highest award because it reflects the deepest formation.

Missionary Practical Guide

This section is written directly for you — the ECYD missionaries who lead these clubs week after week. Most of you are between 20 and 26 years old, from different states across the US or from other countries. You may be serving in DC, Maryland, and Virginia for the first time, far from home. This guide gives you practical, honest, actionable advice for leading boys’ formation with confidence and joy.

Understanding the Boys You Serve

Age-Based Approach

Ages 8–10: Concrete thinkers, high energy, need structure, physical games, and clear rules. They look up to you as a near-superhero. Use that influence well.

Ages 11–12: Starting abstract thought, peer-conscious, respond to humor and competition. They want to feel like “big kids” — give them responsibility.

Ages 13–15: Self-conscious, test authority, need depth, honesty, and space for hard questions. They secretly want your approval even when they push back.

Cultural Awareness (DC/MD/VA)

Families are highly educated, diverse, and politically engaged. Boys are heavily influenced by social media, gaming, and peer pressure. Many are overscheduled with sports and academics.

The LTP may be the only place someone asks about their soul, not their performance. Do not compete for “coolness” — offer what no other activity can: authentic brotherhood and real prayer.

If English is your second language, do not be embarrassed. Your accent is a witness to the universal Church. Speak clearly and ask for help when needed — humility in language is itself a powerful lesson.

Practical Leadership

⚡

Energy & Presence

  • Arrive 20 min early. Pray before boys arrive.
  • Your energy sets the ceiling for the room
  • Stand up, move around, use your hands
  • Learn every boy’s name by Session 2
  • Smile, laugh, be joyful — faith is not grim
📜

Discipline & Tough Moments

  • Correct privately, never publicly
  • One warning, then redirect energy with a task
  • Never use sarcasm or ridicule — they are poison
  • Separate conflicts, hear both sides
  • Boys test you early — respond with calm consistency
💖

Your Spiritual Health

  • Daily prayer is not optional — 15 min minimum
  • Go to confession monthly
  • Find one person to be honest with
  • Rest on your day off — you are human, not a machine
  • Celebrate small wins — they add up over years

Working with Parents & the RC Priest

Parents Are Your Partners

Send the parent message the same day, every week, without exception. It takes 2 minutes and builds enormous trust. If a parent raises a concern, listen first, don’t get defensive, and follow up within 48 hours. Invite parents to observe a session early on — they become your biggest advocates. Remember: trusting their son to a 22-year-old from another state requires real faith. Honor it.

Working with Fr. Vito & RC Priests

The priest is your spiritual father in this mission. Brief him before each session: the saint, Scripture, virtue, and where his presence would be most impactful. Give him clear moments: opening blessing, catechetical connection, confession invitation, commissioning. After the session, share which boys or families need pastoral attention. If he can’t attend, reference him: “Fr. Vito asked me to remind you…”
